HOW DEMONS INFILREATE MINISTRY

How do witches and demons infiltrate people and ministry?

1. Relaxed, Complacent, Lukewarm

Proverbs 1:32
For the turning away of the simple will slay them, And the complacency of fools will destroy them; But whoever listens to me will dwell safely and will be secure, without fear of evil.”

Ministry is ineffective when the people in the Ministry are complacent.
What are the major causes of complacency in ministry?
When the core function of the ministry is delivered, when it's not made known, or when it's unclear. When the vision of the ministry is not understood. There is no direction given to the believer; this can cause complacency, lukewarmness, and relaxation. There are no deliverables; there are no benchmarks to work toward.

2. Confinement

"Confined to the systems of this world"
Caught up in a loop, How many people are out there confined and conflicted within themselves because they dont know how to address situations? Fear to speak up because they fear what may come of it... Witches infiltrate confined areas... Things that cannot be spoken of Our commitment to leaders: some leaders manipulate the truth for self, and their own agenda. In some instances we are "sold a false gospel," one that expects constant sacrifice to man instead of obedience to the voice of God. A gospel that teaches if you don't do and do and do that there is no place for you in the Kingdom, that growth is not eminent for you, and that you won't be able to succeed out of the order of the walls of the church.

The word of God being prostituted to suit man's agenda...
Witches have infiltrated through our very own leadership. People don't even know how to spot witchcraft or discern because they are taught a watered-down, limited gospel. What's worst is that they won't search the truth out for themselves. Instead, feed off a pulpit that is created to build its own vision as opposed to building as the church at large. The people in the pews have limited access to exercise the gifts they have because they are controlled about where they should and should not go. The fires of people are burning out. The minds are controlled and taken over; some churches are preaching freedom yet keeping the members confined.

I do understand that the Shepherd is accountable for the flock and that He leads His flock accordingly, teaches and sends the flock, raises them up to become builders, and doesn't keep them locked in having the lights go out.

3. Relationship with God (intimacy)

Lack of prayer leads to lack of discernment. Lack of fellowship—we are made to have fellowship and be in union with each other. God himself in heaven has Jesus and the Holy Spirit. That is the union of the trinity, so what makes us different that we should be alone? It is the enemy that pulls us into places where we find ourselves alone. Yes, I know there are times we should make time to be alone; those times are specific to prayer times and fellowship with the Holy Spirit, but there are times when we withdraw from people because of offense. This is the work of the enemy; don't listen to that voice. Lack of covering each other in prayer, Lack of Identity, Not understanding purpose or what you have been called to. If you don't have fellowship with the Holy Spirit, how will you know the plans that He has for you? I am not talking about going to church and hearing the pastor teach you what God has for you or the prophet tell you this is what you are called to; I am talking about having a personal relationship.

4. Church hopping: "Not all churches are prostituting the gospel." There is still sound teaching out there. Use the discernment God has given you and ask Him to direct your path.

It is important to be grounded in the place that the Lord places you, "in order to learn and grow and flourish, it is important to be planted in a family-based church." When you jump from church to church, it's easy to learn all different doctrines. it is a dangerous place to be in. Pray about where God should place you so that you can flourish. Don't get caught up in church offenses; these are the things that trap many believers and prevent them.
