GOD WILL USE A MAN

God will use a man!

Joseph was sold to slavery and ended up in prison by His brothers.
He didn't know that from the pit he will receive a place in the palace God used Potiphar, "an Egyptian officer of Pharaoh," to raise him up.
From the pit to the palace.

"God used an Egyptian to raise up an Israelite," a different race group... There is no discrimination with our God.

Moses was born in a time when a decree was sent out: all baby boys had to die. Pharaoh, the king at the time, was trying to protect His crown.

God used Pharaoh's daughter; she found Moses in a river wrapped in swaddling clothes, despite the command that went out by her own father to have every boy killed. She went against all odds and raised up Moses, who later led the Israelites out of Egypt.

"God will use someone, anyone—'Pharoah's daughter.'"
He will raise you up in the presence of your enemies.

The presence of God met Saul at a place called Damascus, where he had a real encounter with God. God took away his eyesight; this was part of God's plan in order for the plan and purpose in Saul's life to be fulfilled. God sent Anananias to help in Saul's recovery. Saul's name changed; he later became Paul. God used a man...God uses people." Anananias is a mighty man of God. He met Saul and helped him on his journey. Paul's ministry was birthed at that place.

The Shunnamite woman and her husband prepared a room for the prophet Elisha. Whenever he passed through the village on his missions, the man of God needed a place to stay.

She understood what it is to honour the man of God; she was a simple yet smart and yes, a "married woman," yet God used her to bless the man of God... God is still using married woman to honour men of God this day. when the assignment is understood, it dont matter gender, God looks at the heart and provides grace for the assignment.

Rahab, the prostitute woman who is in the genealogy of Christ, the mother of Boaz and grandmother of King David, was the woman that hid the two Israelite spies; they were going to be killed by the soldiers of Jericho.

God used a prostitute...
God is no respecter of persons.
He will use a person; His word says that He takes the foolish things of this world to confound the wise.

God raised up Ruth to continue in her journey with Naomi; Naomi lost both her sons. Ruth lost her husband; according to the law, she was free to go, yet she stayed with Naomi. The second daughter-in-law, Olpah, left, but Ruth remained. Ruth was a young attractive woman; she could've continued on her own yet God placed it in her to look after her mother-in-law, and in turn she was blessed with Boaz. Everything that was lost, was restored to Ruth and Naomi This God we serve will use your mother-in-law, the poor old lady, the prostitute, the person that was set out to kill you, and and and...

The purpose of this message teaches us that we should not count any person out, that we should not disregard anyone; you just don't know when God will raise that person up. You just don't know who God will use. These are the different types of people that were used by God as mentioned in each story. These stories have a deep message and show that God is no respecter of persons.

I am reminded of the many powerful men and women of God, yet all so humble. They were used powerfully to pray me into my destiny; they carried me when I couldn't carry myself...

God will use anybody; dont be surprised when it's an old lady that looks like life has left her and she's got nothing left to offer.

Don't be surprised when that 4-year-old tells you, "Mom, God said, 'Do not be afraid.'"

Don't be surprised when you pass a person that appears to be a person of less interest on the street, yet words of wisdom flow out of him because you "in that moment" needed a word from God.

That person sweeping the side of the road today turns out to be the next president.

Stop thinking too highly of yourself; it's not because you have it all that it means that others should be frowned upon.

If God wants to get a message across, He will use whoever and whatever. So don't be so quick to hand anyone over, or to judge anyone, or to persecute anyone. God didn't give up on you; God sent someone to help you on your journey...remember that God will use a man!

And when all else failed He came in the form of a man!!! Jesus

The Shulamite.
