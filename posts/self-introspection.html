SELF-INTROSPECTION

Genesis 22:9-12 NKJV

[9] Then they came to the place of which God had told him. And Abraham built an altar there and placed the wood in order; and he bound Isaac his son and laid him on the altar, upon the wood. [10] And Abraham stretched out his hand and took the knife to slay his son. [11] But the Angel of the Lord called to him from heaven and said, “Abraham, Abraham!” So, he said, “Here I am.” [12] And He said, “Do not lay your hand on the lad, or do anything to him; for now, I know that you fear God, since you have not withheld your son, your only son, from Me.”
I want to ask a few questions that I ask each of you this day to ponder upon and answer within your own heart, I'm asking us to self-reflect to see where we are at on this journey.

Are we willing to make a sacrifice if God spoke today?
We all know that Isaac was Abraham's only child, what if the sacrifice costed you everything?
Are we all spiritually at a place to identify when we are being tested?
Do we have discernment to know if the angel stopped you that you would hear him?
"Abraham already had the knife over his son, it was a split second later when the angel stopped him."

Sometimes God will test our hearts to see if we have the fear of Him, to see if we are willing to go all the way despite what it may look like or feel like... His always checking our hearts to see who can I send? Who can I trust? Who is willing to go despite what i may look like?

If you have answered everything knowing that you are truly living a sacrificial life unto God in which ever area you see yourself, glory be to God.

If there are areas that you could never let go of, I urge you to take it to God in prayer and ask the Holy Spirit to turn the stoney heart to flesh... Most of us are on this road a long time and in the length of our time many setbacks, hurts, expectations and so forth that have hindered our growth and became a stumbling block to move forward.... God wants to put us back on the right path but in order for divine healing we have to yield our hearts to Him once more.
