PERSISTENT IN PRAYER

Luke 18:1-8 AMPC

[1] ALSO [Jesus] told them a parable to the effect that they ought always to pray and not to turn coward (faint, lose heart, and give up).

[2] He said, In a certain city there was a judge who neither reverenced nor feared God nor respected nor considered man.

Now we know that God is our Judge!!!
in the courts of Heaven according to the jurisdiction order of Heaven...

The characteristics of the judge mentioned in this parable tell me that this is not a godly person. When I think about what rules over some communities, I see corruption, violence, exploitation, drug addictions,

gang wars, power, and control.
I believe that whatever rules or controls our communities are the powers ruling over them ... so whatever we see ruling our cities, that is the jurisdiction of that place, and this is what has been allowed by the community, especially when we don't pray for our cities and believe in God for a breakthrough. So what we allow will be allowed, and what we disallow will not be allowed or have any legal ground over us.

[3] And there was a widow in that city who kept coming to him and saying, "Protect and defend and give me justice against my adversary."

A widow is someone that has lost her husband and lost a part of herself. She is someone who has lost great loss; she was use of having a companion and friend, and now that part is missing in her life. What are we losing in this life?

Do you think that the widow could get to the judge physically, and yet the scripture says that she kept coming to him? Now I came. I can only imagine what she kept going to him for; this was over an adversary, something that was out to devour her. I am sure the state of the city could have been devouring her.

[4] And for a time he would not, but later he said to himself, "Though I have neither reverence nor fear for God nor respect nor consideration for man,
[5] Yet because this widow continues to bother me, I will defend and protect and avenge her, lest she give me intolerable annoyance and wear me out by her continually coming or, at the last, she comes and rails on me or assaults me or strangles me.

And for a time he did not help her; her persistence wore him out. I believe he was a ruthless man, as explained in scripture so the logical explanation is that she wore him out in prayer She kept going to our High Priest and Judge and Ruler in the heavenlies, and whilst corruption continued in the land, she persistently continued to go to enter her plea to the courts of heaven. And so heaven bombarded earth and gave the unjust judge no rest in His mind for the lawlessness and corruption in the land.

You see, we are heaven's legislators. Bringing order to earth when we enter the courts of heaven and begin to intercede for our land. Sometimes we feel like our prayers are going unanswered...sometimes it feels long and painful... but what do you think is happening as you continue to gage in prayer Wears me out? How does the evil judge wear out?

[6] Then the Lord said, Listen to what the unjust judge says! [7] And will not [our just] God defend and protect and avenge His elect (His chosen ones), who cry to Him day and night? Will He defer them and delay help on their behalf?

Then the Lord said in this parable, Listen to what the unjust judge says... The one and only ruler over all things will hear us when we begin to cry out, Our prayers go to God like a sweet-smelling aroma; He hears and delivers, he softens the heart of others, He goes before us [8] I tell you, He will defend and protect and avenge them speedily. However, when the Son of Man comes, will He find faith on the earth? Now as we step into a time of prayer? Let us remember that it's okay to be persistent in prayer; the Bible says knock and keep knocking.

Persistent people are those that don't give up!!!!
You don't give up" are those that love....

Love for God..."dont give up."
Love for your family...""don't give up."
Love for your neighbors..."dont give up."
Love for community... "Don't give up."
Love for humanity..."dont give up."

John 14:15-17 NKJV
[15] “If you love Me, keep My commandments. [16] And I will pray the Father, and He will give you another Helper, that He may abide with you forever—[17] the Spirit of truth, whom the world cannot receive, because it neither sees Him nor knows Him; but you know Him, for He dwells with you and will be in you.

The Helper is the "Holy Spirit."

The Holy Spirit will teach you how to pray; you cannot receive the Holy Spirit without accepting Jesus as your personal savior. The world cannot receive the Holy Spirit. You must let go of your past life that is perishing and join yourself to His holiness and allow the Holy Spirit to teach you everything you need to know.

Your family. Your community. are all depending on you to make the right choice!!!
Be persistent.
